mudskipper

the amphibious fish

habitat:
mangroves, estuaries, and tidal mudflats
adaptations:
amphibious lifestyle, ability to breathe through their skin, and strong pectoral fins
behavior:
climbing trees, burrowing in mud, and skipping or hopping movements
diet:
small invertebrates, insects, worms, and algae
reproduction:
external fertilization, males build nests, and females lay eggs in the nest
predators:
birds, larger fish, and crabs
species:
around 35 known species
found in:
coastal regions of Africa, Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands
